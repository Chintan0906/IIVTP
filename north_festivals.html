Festivals of North India üéâ

North India celebrates life with vibrant colors, music, and traditions. From the lights of Diwali to the colors of Holi, these festivals unite people through joy, devotion, and cultural pride.

Diwali

The festival of lights, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Homes are decorated with lamps and sweets are shared among families.

Holi

The festival of colors, celebrated with enthusiasm, music, and splashes of colored powder to mark the arrival of spring.

Baisakhi

A harvest festival celebrated in Punjab marking the start of the new harvest season, filled with dancing and feasting.

Lohri

Celebrated in winter to mark the end of cold days. People gather around bonfires, sing songs, and enjoy sweets made of sesame and jaggery.

Eid

Muslim festival celebrated with prayers, charity, and delicious feasts after a month of fasting during Ramadan.

Navratri & Dussehra

Dedicated to Goddess Durga, celebrated with fasting, garba dances, and the symbolic victory of Lord Rama over Ravana.
