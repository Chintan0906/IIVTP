Eastern India celebrates its vibrant culture with unique festivals full of colors, devotion, and traditions. From Durga Puja in Bengal to Chhath Puja in Bihar, these festivals unite communities in joy and reverence.

Durga Puja

The grand festival of West Bengal, celebrating Goddess Durga‚Äôs victory over Mahishasura, with elaborate decorations, pandals, and cultural programs.

Chhath Puja

A Bihar festival dedicated to the Sun God, involving fasting, holy baths, and offering prayers at riverbanks at sunrise and sunset.

Bihu

An Assamese festival marking the arrival of spring, celebrated with dance, music, and feasting on traditional delicacies.

Magh Bihu

A harvest festival in Assam celebrated with bonfires, feasting, and traditional games at the end of the harvesting season.

Pohela Boishakh

The Bengali New Year, celebrated in West Bengal and Bangladesh with fairs, cultural programs, and traditional food.

Rath Yatra

A grand chariot festival of Lord Jagannath in Puri, Odisha, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists every year.
